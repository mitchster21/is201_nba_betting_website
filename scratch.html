Why the NBA?

I started following the NBA when I was around ten years old. My dad had a worn-out VHS tape of the 1997 Finals, and watching Michael Jordan play defense like an art form made me understand that basketball wasn't just a sport — it was a chess match played at full sprint.

What keeps me hooked today is the intersection of data and drama. Every possession has dozens of measurable variables. Every coaching decision is a hypothesis test. The playoffs especially feel like a five-round statistical convergence — variance compresses, the best teams show up, and the data nerds get proven right (or spectacularly wrong).

The hardwood is where math meets magic.

Quick Facts About Me & The NBA

  1. My Favorite Eras
    • The 90s — physical defense, hand-checking era
    • Early 2000s Big Man basketball (Shaq, Duncan)
    • The current 3-point revolution
  2. Stats I Actually Track
    • True Shooting % (TS%) — best efficiency metric
    • Box Plus/Minus (BPM) — overall impact
    • Net Rating — points differential per 100 possessions
    • ATS Record — beats the spread? You're legit.
  3. Playoff Rules I Live By
    • Home teams are overvalued early in series
    • Road teams that covered in G1 are +EV in G2
    • Fatigue shows up hardest in 4th quarters of G5+
  4. Favorite Playoff Moments (All-Time)
    • Ray Allen's corner three — 2013 Finals G6
    • Kawhi's buzzer beater — 2019 ECF G2
    • LeBron's block — 2016 Finals G7
